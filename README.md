## Project Overview

This repository contains the materials developed for my capstone project in the MBA in Data Science, Artificial Intelligence and Analytics (University of São Paulo – USP/ESALQ).

The project integrates **Evidence-Based Medicine (EBM), Clinical Epidemiology, Psychometrics, Artificial Intelligence, and Health Data Science** to investigate the identification of children and adolescents with ADHD, Giftedness, and Twice-Exceptionality (2e).

Rather than developing a diagnostic algorithm, the project aims to build a rigorous methodological framework capable of supporting future data-driven research and clinical decision support systems.

---

## Research Motivation

Children with giftedness frequently remain unidentified or receive delayed recognition because their cognitive strengths may mask neurodevelopmental disorders.

Conversely, ADHD or other developmental conditions may conceal giftedness, leading to underidentification of twice-exceptional individuals.

Current screening and assessment approaches are heterogeneous, often relying on isolated clinical observations or psychometric instruments with variable methodological quality.

This project seeks to contribute to more reliable and evidence-based approaches for early identification by integrating systematic evidence synthesis with data science methodologies.

---

## Main Objectives

- Conduct a systematic review following PRISMA guidelines.
- Evaluate psychometric properties of screening and assessment instruments.
- Identify clinical and cognitive variables associated with ADHD and giftedness.
- Organize extracted variables into a structured research database.
- Develop a conceptual data model for future machine learning applications.
- Promote reproducible research using open scientific practices.
